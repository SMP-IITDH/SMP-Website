Welcome to DAMP!

The student mentorship program is not just limited to the freshers. The SMP is actually divided into two major branches. The Institute Student Mentorship Program (ISMP) for the freshers, and the Department Academic Mentorship Program (DAMP) for the sophomores. Lets take a dive into the working of the DAMP.

#Department Academic Mentorship Program

This program is a branch of the Student Mentorship Program and was started in the year 2020-21. As we know, the courses and activities of students turns mostly branch specific as they move on to become sophomores. This program aims to help the students make a smooth transition across academic semesters and get invaluable advice from their seniors. Every year, a list of seniors specialising in various fields and across all the branches are chosen to become DAMP mentors, so as to help students explore and choose their areas of interest by discussing with the DAMP mentors. The DAMP mentors disseminate information relating to their branch and guide their mentees about suitable academic choices, prospective career options, etc. The DAMP mentors are not restricted just to mentees of their branch. It provides flexibility for the mentees to ask questions and get suitable guidance as they might have interests lying in other branches too. The DAMP is not year-specific either, the freshers can always contact the DAMP mentors and get as much benefit as they can out of it if they are really interested in a specific topic a mentor specialises in. All in all, the combination of the ISMP and DAMP helps provide the sophomores a sense of responsibility of guiding the juniors while not being left alone in the dark, as they are guided by the DAMP mentors into a brighter future.
